UIengineering Weekly – 28/2018

UIengineering,Design Systems,Frontend development

<p>This is a summary of interesting articles I found and read this week. You can follow along during the week on Twitter with the <a href="https://twitter.com/search?q=%23UIengineering">#UIengineering</a> hashtag and in the <a href="https://www.reddit.com/r/UIengineering/">UIengineering subreddit</a>.</p> <h2><a href="https://www.smashingmagazine.com/2018/07/pattern-library-first-css/">Pattern Library First: An Approach For Managing CSS</a></h2> <p>Rachel Andrew wrote this practical article about building up a pattern library:</p> <blockquote> <p>CSS can be hard to manage across a project, especially when you need to include media queries for various breakpoints and fallbacks for older browsers. In this article, we will take a look at using Fractal to manage components which use CSS Grid.</p> </blockquote> <h2><a href="https://medium.com/@broccolini/design-systems-at-github-c8e5378d2542">Design Systems at GitHub</a></h2> <p>Diana Mounter manages the Design System team at GitHub. In this article she shares their story of how they started out, evolved the system and scaled their team.</p> <h2><a href="http://jxnblk.com/writing/posts/defining-component-apis-in-react/">Defining Component APIs in React</a></h2> <p>Brent Jackson shares his learnings from building components. Even though the examples are made with React, most of them can be applied to other component libraries as well:</p> <blockquote> <p>The following is a collection of thoughts, opinions, and advice for defining component APIs that are meant to be more flexible, composable, and easier to understand.</p> </blockquote> <h2><a href="https://adactio.com/journal/14103">Components and concerns</a></h2> <p>Jeremy Keith reflects separation of concerns when building components:</p> <blockquote> <p>In the case of pages, asking “what is the core functionality?” will help you come up with a good URL. With components, asking “what is the core functionality?” will help you come up with a good name …</p> </blockquote>
